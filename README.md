# Einsteinian Sandbox

A browser-based interactive visualization of Einstein's
universe — from the core reversal of Newtonian physics
to the edge of what current mathematics can describe.

No download. No install. Open it and feel where the
map ends.

## What It Is

Newton said matter moves through space and time.
Einstein said matter tells space and time how to curve —
and curved space and time tell matter how to move.

That is not a refinement. That is a different universe.

There is no force pulling you to Earth right now.
You are following the straightest possible path through
curved spacetime. That path looks like falling.

Einsteinian Sandbox makes that visible across four tiers —
from the comparison of two frameworks, through the
manipulation of time and space, to the boundary of
what human knowledge can currently see.

## Tiers

**Tier I — The Reversal**
Two canvases side by side. Newton on the left —
a flat, neutral grid, objects moving through space
with force acting at a distance, Kepler's elliptical
orbits accelerating near periapsis exactly as the
math predicts.

Einstein on the right — the same objects, the same
orbits, the same accelerations. But the grid bends.
Space itself curves around the central mass. The
objects aren't being pulled. They are following
the shape of the fabric.

The field equations sit in the panel alongside a
plain-language translation. E=mc² gets the same
treatment — not just a formula, but a statement
that mass and energy were always one thing.

**Tier II — Time**
Everything frozen except time's relationship to mass.

Two clocks. One near the mass, one far away. Dial
the mass up and watch them diverge. Not because
something is wrong with the clocks. Because time
itself passes at different rates depending on where
you are in a gravitational field.

A twin paradox visualization shows two travelers —
one stationary, one moving. Adjust the speed and
watch the age gap grow. At 99% the speed of light,
the traveler ages 7 times slower than the one who
stayed behind.

The GPS anchor sits in the panel — satellites at
20,200 km experience time 38 microseconds faster
per day than clocks on Earth's surface. Without
constant correction, GPS drifts 11 kilometers daily.
This is not theoretical. It is running right now
in orbit above you.

**Tier III — Space**
Time locked. Mass is the pin. The fabric is yours.

Click anywhere to place a mass object. Drag it.
Stack multiple masses and watch the fabric warp
between them. The grid lines respond in real time —
brighter where curvature is steepest, dimmer where
space is flat.

Toggle light paths and watch them trace curves
through the fabric — not because light bends,
but because straight lines through curved space
look bent to any flat observer. This was confirmed
in 1919 during a solar eclipse. It was the first
experimental proof that Einstein was right.

Toggle frame dragging and watch space itself rotate
around a spinning mass like honey around a spoon.
Measured by Gravity Probe B in 2011 after 40 years
of development.

Increase mass past 70 and the event horizon forms —
an accretion disk glows around a geometry that has
closed inward completely. Not a surface. Not a wall.
A shape of space from which there is no outward
direction left.

**Tier IV — The Ten Dimensions**
This is where the map ends.

D0 through D10. Each dimension has a sky, a ground,
a sphere that changes color as you grow, and the
previous dimension wrapped around it — shrinking
as you expand into the next one looming above you,
too large to understand until you're inside it.

D3 is home. The world you know.
D4 — time as a physical axis. Three clocks spin
     forward, backward, and randomly.
D5 — Kaluza-Klein. Ghost duplicates of the scene
     drift apart as the fifth dimension folds space.
D6 — String theory. Twelve objects flicker in and
     out of existence along vibrating strings.
D7 — M-theory. Impossible geometry. A brane surface.
     Our universe as a 3-surface in an 11D bulk.
D8 — Octonions. An 8-fold rosette cycling through
     color space. The mathematics is beautiful.
     The physics is unresolved.
D9 — The limit. Three interference patterns overlay
     the scene. Patterns without names.
D10 — White noise. The sphere turns black.
      This is the honest answer.
      Here is where representation ends.

The grow and shrink buttons dissolve one dimension
into the next. The transition is slow because you
are not traveling to these dimensions. You are
becoming aware of them.

## Part of the Sandbox Series

series of interactive science visualizations designed
to make complex systems feel real, explorable, and
personally meaningful.
